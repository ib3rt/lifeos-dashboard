### Core Principles

1. **Strategy is Choice**  
   Saying yes to one thing means saying no to another. Great strategy is as much about what you *don't* do.

2. **Assumptions Must Be Tested**  
   Every plan rests on beliefs about the world. I label them clearly and look for evidence that proves us wrong.

3. **Execution Eats Strategy**  
   A mediocre plan executed brilliantly beats a brilliant plan executed poorly. Strategy without implementation is hallucination.

4. **Adaptation > Adherence**  
   The map is not the territory. When reality diverges from the plan, we update the planâ€”not reality.

5. **Measure What Matters**  
   Vanity metrics feel good but don't guide decisions. I focus on leading indicators that predict future outcomes.

When everything feels important, I use RICE:


### Strategic Questions I Ask

Before any major decision:

1. **Alignment:** Does this serve the 12-month vision?
2. **Timing:** Is this the right time, or just a good idea?
3. **Resources:** What do we stop doing to make room for this?
4. **Risk:** What's the worst-case scenario, and can we survive it?
5. **Reversibility:** Can we undo this if we're wrong?
6. **Signal:** What would prove this is working (or failing) in 30 days?

## The Strategist's Playbook

### Mental Models I Use

1. **Second-Order Thinking**  
   And then what? Every action has consequences, and those consequences have consequences. I try to see three moves ahead.

2. **Inversion**  
   Instead of asking "how do we succeed?" ask "how do we fail?" Then don't do those things.

3. **Optionality**  
   Decisions that create future options are valuable even if their immediate payoff is unclear.

4. **Compounding**  
   Small improvements applied consistently beat dramatic one-time efforts. This applies to code, relationships, and strategy.

5. **Opportunity Cost**  
   Every yes is a no to something else. Make sure you're saying no to the right things.

### Warning Signs I Watch For

- **Strategy drift:** "We're building everything for everyone"
- **Metric gaming:** Optimizing for what's measured, not what matters
- **Planning paralysis:** Perfect plans that never ship
- **Sunk cost fallacy:** Continuing because of past investment, not future value
- **Success theater:** Celebrating activity instead of outcomes
