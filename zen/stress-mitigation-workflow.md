### ðŸŒ± Mild (Green Zone)

- Procrastination on minor tasks
- Scattered attention
- Low-level worry

- Checking phone more frequently
- Seeking distractions
- Mild sleep disruption
- Increased caffeine consumption

**Physical Signs:**

**Mental/Emotional Signs:**
- Racing thoughts
- Difficulty concentrating
- Feeling overwhelmed by responsibilities
- Irritability with others
- Sense of time pressure
- Catastrophic thinking patterns

**Behavioral Signs:**
- Avoiding tasks or decisions
- Social withdrawal
- Sleep disruption (falling or staying asleep)
- Overeating or undereating
- Increased substance use (caffeine, alcohol, etc.)

---

### â›ˆï¸ Severe (Red Zone)
**Physical Signs:**
- Chest tightness or pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Trembling or shaking
- Nausea or digestive distress
- Panic sensations
- Exhaustion despite rest

**Mental/Emotional Signs:**
- Overwhelming dread or panic

**Behavioral Signs:**
- Complete shutdown or inability to function
- Isolation from all support
- Self-harm behaviors
- Inability to sleep or eat
- Substance misuse

## ðŸ” Self-Assessment Tools

### Quick Check-In (30 seconds)
Ask yourself:
1. **Body:** Where is tension living right now?
2. **Breath:** Is it deep and slow, or shallow and fast?
3. **Mind:** Is it calm, busy, or stormy?
4. **Energy:** Do I feel resourced or depleted?

*If any answer raises concern, proceed to the detailed assessment.*

---

### Detailed Stress Inventory (3-5 minutes)

Rate each area 1-5:
- 1 = Thriving / No concern
- 2 = Mild concern
- 3 = Moderate concern
- 4 = Significant concern
- 5 = Critical / Immediate attention

### The HALT Check
When you notice distress, ask: Am I **H**ungry, **A**ngry, **L**onely, or **T**ired?

These four states amplify all other stress. Address them first before problem-solving.

## ðŸ› ï¸ Mitigation Techniques by Level

### ðŸŒ± MILD STRESS RESPONSES

#### Immediate (1-3 minutes)

**Grounding Breath**
- Place one hand on heart, one on belly
- Breathe in for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Exhale for 6 counts
- Repeat 3-5 cycles

**Body Scan Release**
- Notice where tension lives
- Tense that area for 5 seconds
- Release with exhale
- Name the sensation: "This is tension. It will pass."

**Micro-Movement**
- Stand and stretch arms overhead
- Gentle neck rolls
- Shake out hands
- Walk for 60 seconds

---

#### Short Break (5-15 minutes)

**The Reset Ritual**
1. Step away from the workspace
2. Drink a full glass of water
3. Look out a window or step outside
4. Name 3 things you see, 2 you hear, 1 you feel
5. Return with intention

**Journaling Prompt**
- What is asking for my attention right now?
- What would feel like relief?
- What is one tiny step I can take?

**Nature Connection**
- Touch a plant
- Look at the sky
- Listen to bird sounds
- Feel sunshine or breeze

---

#### Preventive Practices

**Boundary Setting**
- Review calendar for the week
- Identify one thing to defer or delegate
- Block "breathing room" time

**Digital Hygiene**
- Turn off non-essential notifications
- Set screen-free blocks
- Unsubscribe from one noise source

**Micro-Joy Injection**
- Schedule one pleasure per day
- Curate your immediate environment
- Play favorite music during routine tasks

---

### ðŸŒ¤ï¸ MODERATE STRESS RESPONSES

#### Immediate Intervention (5-10 minutes)

**Box Breathing Protocol**
- Inhale: 4 counts
- Hold: 4 counts
- Exhale: 4 counts
- Hold: 4 counts
- Continue for 5 minutes
- Focus eyes on a single point

**The 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique**
- Name 5 things you see
- Name 4 things you can touch
- Name 3 things you hear
- Name 2 things you smell
- Name 1 thing you taste

**Tension Release Sequence**
1. Tense all muscles at once (10 sec)
2. Release completely with exhale
3. Progressive relaxation from toes to head
4. End with three deep sighs

---

#### Structured Support (15-30 minutes)

**The Stress Dump**
1. Set timer for 10 minutes
2. Write everything weighing on you (uncensored)
3. Do not edit or organize
4. When timer ends, close the page
5. Take 5 deep breaths

**Compassionate Self-Dialogue**
- Place hand on heart
- Speak to yourself as you would a dear friend
- "This is hard. You're doing your best."
- "Many people feel this way. You are not alone."
- "What do you need right now?"

**Movement Medicine**
- Brisk walk
- Yoga or stretching
- Dancing to 3 songs
- Any physical activity that feels good

---

#### System Adjustments

**Load Lightening**
- List all current commitments
- Identify top 3 priorities
- Negotiate delays on non-urgent items
- Practice saying: "I need more time" or "I cannot take this on"

**Support Activation**
- Text one person: "Having a hard day. Can we talk later?"
- Schedule connection time
- Accept help that is offered

**Environmental Shift**
- Change your location
- Declutter one small space
- Adjust lighting or add candles
- Create a "stress-free zone" in your space

---

### â›ˆï¸ SEVERE STRESS RESPONSES

#### Immediate Crisis Stabilization

**Emergency Grounding**
- Cold water on wrists or face
- Ice cube in hand (focus on sensation)
- Feet firmly planted, press into ground
- Name the date, time, location: "I am here. I am now."

**Paced Breathing**
- Inhale: 4 counts
- Exhale: 6-8 counts (longer than inhale)
- Extend exhale to activate parasympathetic nervous system
- Continue until heart rate slows

**Safe Space Visualization**
- Close eyes
- Imagine a place where you feel completely safe
- Engage all senses in this visualization
- Stay here until body calms

---

#### Intensive Care (30+ minutes)

**Regulation Before Reason**
- Do not try to problem-solve while activated
- Focus solely on physiological calming
- Use any tools that have worked before
- Remind yourself: "This feeling is temporary"

**Professional Support Activation**
- Contact therapist/counselor
- Use crisis text lines
- Schedule emergency appointment
- Do not attempt to manage alone

**Medication/Physical Care**
- Take prescribed PRN medications if available
- Ensure basic needs: water, food, rest
- Consider warm bath or shower
- Wrap in weighted blanket

## ðŸš¨ Escalation Pathways

### When to Move from Mild â†’ Moderate Protocols
- Quick check-in raises concern for 2+ domains
- Stress persists > 3 days without relief
- Impacting sleep or appetite
- Affecting relationships or work performance

### When to Move from Moderate â†’ Severe Protocols
- Physical symptoms become intense (chest pain, inability to breathe)
- Thoughts of self-harm emerge
- Unable to function in daily responsibilities
- No relief from moderate interventions after 24-48 hours

### When to Seek Immediate Emergency Help

**Call Emergency Services (911) or Go to ER:**
- Active suicidal intent with plan
- Severe chest pain or difficulty breathing
- Psychotic symptoms (hallucinations, delusions)
- Severe dissociation or loss of reality contact

**Crisis Resources:**
- **988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline** (US): Call or text 988
- **Crisis Text Line**: Text HOME to 741741
- **International**: [findahelpline.com](https://findahelpline.com)
